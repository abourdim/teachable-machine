Cold outreach templates — English

Direct-sales templates for emailing STEM teachers, makerspace coordinators, homeschool co-ops, and district tech buyers. Paired templates in OUTREACH-fr.md and OUTREACH-ar.md.

Deliverability tips

  • Send from a personal domain, not Gmail. Teachers' spam filters nuke @gmail.com cold email.
  • One email per contact per week, max. Stop if they don't reply twice.
  • Don't attach the ZIP. Link to the live demo (abourdim.github.io/teachable-machine) — it qualifies them and saves your outbox.
  • For US K-12: address as Ms./Mr. [Last name]. For UK: Mr./Ms. [Last name]. For makerspaces: first name is fine.

Subject-line bank

Rotate subject lines — repeating the same one into the same organisation's spam filter tanks delivery.

Curiosity:

  • A browser-based micro:bit tool for your classroom (no install)
  • The micro:bit tool that saved one teacher a whole class period
  • Tried this on Chromebooks yet?

Direct benefit:

  • Zero-install control panel for your micro:bits
  • Live sensors in the browser — no IDE, no setup
  • A STEM tool that pairs on a Chromebook in 30 seconds

Question-based:

  • How are you running micro:bit in the classroom?
  • Does your co-op use micro:bits?
  • Quick question about your {{SUBJECT}} unit

Low-commitment:

  • 30-second demo before you decide
  • Free copy if you'd like to try it with a class
  • No pitch — just a tool you might find useful
