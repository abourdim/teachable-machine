Testimonial templates — English

Three messages, sent in order, 3-7 days apart. Adapt tone to the buyer (teacher vs maker vs homeschool parent). Overview + rules in TESTIMONIALS.md.

Showcase ask (day 7)

Subject: One favour — can I see it in action?

Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

Hope Teachable Machine has been working in the classroom. One thing that would mean a huge amount if you're up for it: a photo of your students using it.

I'm a tiny one-person shop and the biggest thing holding me back is that new buyers can't see real classrooms using the tool. A single photo + a two-sentence quote from you would go further than a hundred dollars of Etsy ads.

If you're open to it:

  1. Snap one photo — even a wide shot of the classroom with the LED matrix lit up is perfect. Student faces not required (and honestly easier on everyone).
  2. Send me a 2-sentence note on what worked (or didn't).

I'll send you a free sticker pack with your school name on it as a thank-you, either way.

No worries if this isn't a yes — your review on Etsy already helps more than you know.

— {{YOUR_NAME}}

Permission-to-repost waiver

Subject: Quick waiver so I can share your photo properly

Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

Thank you SO much for the photo and quote — they're genuinely great.

Before I post them anywhere, quick legal bit (I have to ask, even when I don't want to):

Can you confirm by replying "I grant permission" that:

  • The photo you sent is yours (you took it, or it was taken at an event where photography was permitted)
  • I may repost it on social media (Etsy, Pinterest, Instagram, Twitter), in my press kit, on the product landing page, and in email to other teachers
  • You are a {{TEACHER / LIBRARIAN / PARENT / MAKERSPACE ORGANISER}} and can make this decision for the setting shown
  • If identifiable students appear, you've confirmed parental consent is on file per your institution's policy
  • You'd like to be credited as: {{PREFERRED_CREDIT}} (example: "Ms. Sarah K., 5th-grade STEM teacher, Austin TX" — first-name-only is totally fine)

If any of those are a "no," tell me what is and isn't OK and I'll adapt. The default is: I use the photo with the credit above. You can revoke any time — just message me and I pull it.

Thanks again — this really does move the needle for a tiny shop.

— {{YOUR_NAME}}

Thank-you + reward

Subject: Your sticker pack + a gift I wasn't planning to send

Hey {{FIRST_NAME}},

Waiver received — thank you. Three things:

  1. Your sticker pack (with {{SCHOOL_NAME}}'s name on it) is attached / linked below.
  2. Bonus: here's a coupon for 50% off the next major version when it ships — THANKS-{{FIRST_NAME_UPPER}}-{{YYMM}}. Single-use. Never expires.
  3. What I'm doing with your photo: posting on Pinterest this week with your credit line, and it'll go on my press kit page by next month. Happy to send you the Pinterest link when it's live so you can share it with your co-teachers.

If you want to stay in the loop on new versions (new sensors, new 3D models, etc.), reply "yes updates" and I'll ping you when 1.3.0 ships. Otherwise I won't bother you.

Thanks again, — {{YOUR_NAME}}

Quote extraction (optional helper)

When a teacher replies with a long message, extract a pull-quote by asking their permission to use a specific shorter form:

Hi {{FIRST_NAME}} — one tiny thing before I share this anywhere. Out of what you wrote, this one sentence jumped out:

"{{PULL_QUOTE}}"

Two questions:

  1. OK to use it exactly like that?
  2. Any tweak you'd prefer? Grammar, tone, anything — just say the word and I'll swap it.

I'll wait for your thumbs-up before posting anywhere.

— {{YOUR_NAME}}
